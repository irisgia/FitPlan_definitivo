HOW TO SPLIT YOUR SCHEDULE?

 
When deciding how to split your schedule, you should consider some key elements:
 
- How many days per week you want to train
- Set the right amount of recovery time
- Your goal
 
Based on these elements, there are different way to split you schedule:
 
 
 
 

TOTAL BODY

On each training session you will train every major muscle.
 
The training schedule is usually divided on three days and each training day is alternated with a rest one.
 
It is extremely suited for beginner since it allows them to increase the general fitness level while reducing the next-day muscle soreness.
 
Typical example is:
 
  Mon - Total Body
  Tue - Rest day
  Wed - Total Body
  Thu - Rest day
  Fri - Total Body
  Sat and Sun - Rest days
 
The Total Body schedule is usually comprised of 6 to 8 exercises (10-12 reps per 3 sets).
 
The training days can be split as well as: Tue/Thu/Sat (Rest days: Mon/Wed/Fri/Sun)

UPPER BODY
&
LOWER BODY

It usually requires training 4 days per week.
 
  Mon - Upper Body
  Tue - Lower Body
  Wed - Rest day
  Thu - Upper Body
  Fri - Lower Body
  Sat and Sun - Rest days
 
The training days are usually comprised of
6 to 8 exercises (6-8 reps or 10-12 reps per 3-2 sets)
 
and can be split as well as:
Tue/Wed and Fri/Sat (Rest days: Mon/Thu/Sun)
 

THE 4 DAYS SPLIT

It usually pairs big muscles with small ones - the small muscle is already working during many of your big muscle exercises, you simply hit it afterward.
 
  Mon - Chest + Triceps
  Tue - Back + Biceps
  Wed - Rest day
  Thu - Legs
  Fri - Shoulders
  Sat and Sun - Rest days
 
The training days are usually comprised of 4 to 6 exercises (6 to 15 reps per 3-4 sets).
 
The rest time in between sets should always be under 2 minutes and above 30 seconds.
